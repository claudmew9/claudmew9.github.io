Merge

Magazine

Merge magazine is a bi-monthly magazine focused on bringing people together to merge designs to help people improve the future. The magazine was based on a design brief stemming from typography in context.

To make the magazine, a style guide was created to outline properties such as the masthead, grid structure, layout, and fonts.

Find the Palms and Gums

Prototype Installation

Explore and Experience the physical journey of the artist Ellis Rowan. Ellis Rowan is a natural historian interested in Australian Flora and Fauna. She traveled throughout Australia to experience the Australian environment, and her experience is implemented to the visitor in a circular installation where they will find all the palms and gums to receive her book. The visitors will discover the plants through augmented reality. Overall this virtual and physical experience creates an environment for the viewer to have fun while being educated on the Australian palms and gums.

The Beginning and the End

Still Life Photography

The photographs explore the themes of life and death. The natural cycle that I portrayed though the juxtaposition of two opposing concepts; decomposition and vibrancy. The dark and dramatic lighting, which I achieved though the use of light room as well as photoshop, allows me to mimic Dutch still life paintings.

The rotten fruit and flowers create texture and manipulate our perspective to make the natural objects seem alive in a simple, yet confronting way.

Technicolor

Virtual Exhibition

Technicolour is a compilation of all the works from aspiring design students. The central theme of our exhibition is colour and how we use colour to express social and gender views. These are confronting and ignored aspects of society. Gender includes the different perspectives on textile waste, human connection towards space, the loneliness in association with technology, the social status of people, the fear of gender and the mixed connotations towards gender.

Overall, these works have different perspectives to remind the audience about social and gender views in the world.
